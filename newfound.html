Newfoundland Coast

Newfoundland and Labrador is Canada's most easterly province, a place of rugged beauty, unique culture, and incredible hospitality. Its coastline is a breathtaking panorama of dramatic cliffs, icebergs, and picturesque fishing villages. The capital, St. John's, is one of North America's oldest cities, with a vibrant arts scene and the iconic, colorful houses of Jellybean Row.

The province's history is deeply tied to the sea, from the Viking settlement at L'Anse aux Meadows to its long-standing fishing traditions. The Labrador part of the province is a vast, wild territory with a landscape of subarctic tundra and boreal forest. This unique province offers an unforgettable experience, rich with history, natural wonders, and the friendliest people you'll ever meet.
