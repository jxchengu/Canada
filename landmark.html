Provinces & Territories

Ontario

Home to Canada's capital, Ottawa, and its largest city, Toronto. A hub for finance, culture, and government.

Learn more about Ontario →

Alberta

Known for its stunning Rocky Mountain landscapes, Alberta is a province that offers a mix of outdoor adventures and urban experiences. Major cities include Calgary and Edmonton.

Learn more about Alberta →

British Columbia

Famous for its majestic mountains, lush rainforests, and beautiful Pacific coastline. A haven for outdoor enthusiasts.

Learn more about British Columbia →

New Brunswick

This Maritime province is known for its rugged coastline, vast forests, and the dramatic tidal bores of the Bay of Fundy.

Learn more about New Brunswick →

Nova Scotia

Surrounded by the sea, Nova Scotia is a charming Maritime province celebrated for its historic lighthouses, coastal fishing villages, and the scenic Cabot Trail.

Learn more about Nova Scotia →

Prince Edward Island

Canada's smallest province is known for its pastoral landscapes, iconic red-sand beaches, and as the home of Anne of Green Gables.

Learn more about Prince Edward Island →

Quebec

A unique province with a distinct French-Canadian culture, rich history, and charming cities like Montreal and North America's only walled city, Quebec City.

Learn more about Quebec →

Newfoundland and Labrador

The most easterly province, known for its rugged Viking and colonial history, unique dialect, and stunning icebergs and fjords.

Learn more about Newfoundland →

Manitoba

A central prairie province known for its vast farmlands, scenic parks, and a vibrant cultural scene in the city of Winnipeg.

Learn more about Manitoba →

Saskatchewan

Famous for its majestic mountains, lush rainforests, and beautiful Pacific coastline. A haven for outdoor enthusiasts.

Learn more about Saskatchewan →

Nunavut

Nunavut is Canada's newest and largest territory, with a vast Arctic landscape and a culture profoundly shaped by its Inuit heritage.

Learn more about Nunavut →

Northwest Territories

The Northwest Territories are a land of striking contrasts, known for some of Canada's largest lakes and as a premier location to view the Northern Lights.

Learn more about Northwest Territories →

Yukon

The Yukon is a territory steeped in the history of the Klondike Gold Rush and defined by its immense, untamed wilderness.

Learn more about Yukon →
