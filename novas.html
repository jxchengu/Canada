Nova Scotia Coastline

Nova Scotia is one of Canada's three Maritime provinces, a place where life is deeply connected to the sea. The province's name, Latin for "New Scotland," hints at its rich Celtic and Gaelic heritage. With over 13,300 kilometers of coastline, it's famous for its dramatic sea cliffs, picturesque fishing villages, and historic lighthouses, making it a dream destination for coastal drives and whale watching.

The provincial capital, Halifax, is a lively port city with a vibrant arts and music scene, and a storied history. From the historic fortifications of the Halifax Citadel to the fresh seafood served in waterfront restaurants, Nova Scotia offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Its friendly locals and laid-back pace make it a truly welcoming place to explore.
