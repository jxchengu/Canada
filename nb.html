New Brunswick's Bay of Fundy

New Brunswick stands out as Canada's only officially bilingual province, with a strong Acadian and French-speaking culture. This unique blend of English and French heritage gives the province a distinctive character. It's home to the famous Bay of Fundy, which boasts the highest tides in the world, a truly awe-inspiring natural phenomenon.

The province is a haven for outdoor adventures, with vast forests, picturesque rivers, and over 60 covered bridges that are a delight to explore. Major cities like Saint John, Moncton, and the capital, Fredericton, each offer their own charm, from historic architecture to vibrant downtown cores. New Brunswick's natural wonders and cultural richness make it a fascinating place to visit. An interesting fact is that New Brunswick is home to Saint John, the oldest city in Canada and known for getting mixed up with Saint Johns, Newfoundland and Labrador.
