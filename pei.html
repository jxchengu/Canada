Prince Edward Island

The smallest of Canada's provinces, Prince Edward Island is a charming and pastoral destination known for its iconic red-sand beaches, rolling green hills, and historic lighthouses. It is famously the setting for Lucy Maud Montgomery's beloved novel, "Anne of Green Gables," and the island embraces this heritage with museums and attractions dedicated to the story.

PEI's economy is primarily based on agriculture, especially its world-famous potatoes, and a thriving tourism industry. The island's capital, Charlottetown, is a historic city with a walkable downtown and a rich history as the birthplace of the Canadian Confederation. Its laid-back pace and welcoming locals make it an ideal retreat for those seeking a quiet, scenic getaway.
