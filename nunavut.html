Nunavut Tundra

Nunavut, Canada's newest and largest territory, is a land of Arctic beauty and rich Inuit culture. It was created in 1999 to give the Inuit people self-governance and control over their land, making it a place with a unique cultural and political identity. The landscape is a pristine wilderness of frozen tundra, fjords, and ice floes, home to Arctic wildlife like polar bears and caribou.

The territory's capital, Iqaluit, is a small but important community that serves as a cultural and administrative center. Nunavut offers a chance to experience a way of life deeply connected to the land and to witness incredible natural phenomena like the Northern Lights. Its remote location and dramatic landscapes make it a destination for truly intrepid travelers.
